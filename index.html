I am a Computer Science PhD student at Johns Hopkins University. Advised by Prof. Soudeh Ghrobani, my research concentrates on designing techniques to effectively measure and resolve congestion events with microsecond-scale granularity, known as microbursts, that occur in modern datacenters. I received my bachelors degree from Sharif University of Technology. For my Bachelor's thesis, I evaluated the effects of load balancing in Named Data Networking (NDN) architecture.

Research Interests

My main field of research focuses on ways of measuring and resolving microsecond-scale congestion events, known as microbursts, in large datacenters. Currently, I am probing packet deflection, \i.e., re-routing the packets forwarded to a congested port instead of dropping them, as a potential solution to the microburst problem.

In the past, I have spent some time probing Named Data Networking (NDN) as a new architecture proposed for the network layer that tries to resolve the limitations of IP-based architecture using names instead of IPs.
